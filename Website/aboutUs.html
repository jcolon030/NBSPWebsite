Meet The Chapter

Letrell Harris recently graduated Morehouse College Magna Cum Laude with a Bachelor of Science degree in physics and is currently a second year graduate student at Michigan State University (MSU). He currently conducts research in high energy physics at MSU working with the High Altitude Water Cherenkov Collaboration. He is well traveled, his physics has taken him to the University of Hawaii MÃ£noa, Michigan State University, Thomas Jefferson National Laboratory, Lawrence Livermore National Lab, and Los Alamos National Laboratory to name a few. He was a Ronald McNair Scholar, has served as a Charter Member and Chapter President of The Hampton Roads Chapter of The National Society of Black Physicists (NSBP), The Morehouse College Chapter of NSBP, and the Spartan Chapter of NSBP. He was an ambassador for the Morehouse Center for Excellence in Education, a mentor for the Harlem Gallery of Science and NSBP Mentorship Program, a member of the Student Council of NSBP, is currently a MSU College of Natural Science Fellowship recipient, a MSU University Enrichment Fellowship Recipient, and a member of Alpha Phi Alpha Fraternity Incorporated. In his free time, he practices Brazilian Jiu Jitsu and mentors the youth both on and off his campus.
